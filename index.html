Why Rio de Janeiro?

Rio, the city between mountains and sea

Legend says that when Portuguese explorers first arrived at Guanabara Bay in January 1502, they mistook it for the mouth of a great river — hence the name *Rio de Janeiro*. But long before the arrival of Europeans, this land was home to the Tupinambá people, whose deep connection to the forest, the sea, and the sky still echoes through the city’s rhythm.

Today, Rio is a place where nature and urban life embrace each other in dazzling harmony. From the lush peaks of the Tijuca Forest to the golden curves of Copacabana and Ipanema, the city unfolds in layers — of samba, sunsets, street art, colonial churches, and modernist icons. You can hike through the jungle in the morning, sip coconut water by the beach at noon, and watch the sun set behind Christ the Redeemer in the evening.

More than a postcard, Rio is a feeling: warm, intense, and unforgettable. It’s a city that pulses to its own beat — vibrant, imperfect, and full of life.
